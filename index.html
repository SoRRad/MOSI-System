MOSI Clinical Audit Platform — Development, Research & Institutional Support
MOSI Metabolic & Obesity Staging Index

The Metabolic & Obesity Staging Index (MOSI) is a three-dimensional bariatric surgery staging system developed at Mayo Clinic (Rochester, MN) from a derivation cohort of 3,097 patients. MOSI integrates three independent axes — M (BMI class), O (metabolic comorbidity burden), and Si (systemic impairment level) — to assign each patient to a surgical complexity stage (I, II, III, 4A, or 4B) with evidence-based procedure recommendations and validated outcome rates.

The companion MOSI-T Tier System maps each patient's comorbidity profile to a minimum Total Weight Loss target (Tiers A–D, ranging from ≥7.5% to ≥30% TWL) required for metabolic goal achievement. This enables surgeons to set individualized, evidence-based targets and select the procedure most likely to achieve them.

This Clinical Audit Platform provides real-time MOSI decision support and prospectively captures surgeon agreement data, clinical judgements, and actual outcomes — constituting the internal validation dataset for the manuscript in preparation.

🔢 MOSI Algorithm Overview
M Score — BMI Class
M1 (30–34.9) · M2 (35–39.9) · M3 (40–44.9) · M4 (45–49.9) · M5 (≥50 kg/m²)
O Score — Comorbidities
HTN + Dysglycaemia + OSA + Hyperlipidemia (0–4 additive points)
Si Level — Impairment
Si0 (none) · Si1 (microvascular) · Si2 (organ dysfunction) · Si3A/B (organ failure)
MOSI-T Tier System
Tier A ≥7.5% · B ≥15% · C ≥25% · D ≥30% TWL — mapped to comorbidity goals
